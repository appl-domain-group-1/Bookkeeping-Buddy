General Questions About Bookkeeping Buddy

Bookkeeping Buddy is and accounting software designed for users to easily track income, expenses, and more for their accounting needs.


Bookkeeping Buddy comes with a variety of features to help manage accounts and accounting as a whole. It stores data about users and their financial status for secure and easy viewing.



Login Page

All active users of Bookkeeping Buddy come here to login and access their account by signing in with their username and password.


Users that have an account but have forgotten their password can fill out the forgot password page to reset their password. Users will have to enter their E-mail, Username, and answer their three security questions.

Register Page

New users of Bookkeeping Buddy set up their account here. All new users must submit their E-mail, Password, First name, Last name, Address, Date of birth, and answer three security questions. After all information is submitted, an administrator will respond by activating the account within 3-5 business days.



Accounts Page

The Accounts page allows users to view, find, and create accounts to be added to the database.


This button will reveal all accounts related to either assets, liabilities, equities, revenues, or expenses.


Use this button to view all the changes that have occurred to financial accounts.


Create a new account for the server by giving it a name, description, category (asset, liability, equity, etc...), subcategory, debit or credit, inital balance #, statement, and possibly a comment.


Administrators can send E-mails to other users directly from the accounts page using this button. All that's needed it the User's E-mail, a subject, and the message.


An account can either be edited or activated/deactivated. Editing accounts allows users to change any information within an account. Activating or deactivating will change if the account will be counted. Deactivating will not delete an account.



Statements

The Statements drop down leads to the three statement related pages: Income Statements, Retained Earning Statements, and Balance Sheets.


On this page, all documented revenues and expenses are listed and totaled into a Net Income. The Income Statement can be saved or printed with the bottom left button on the page. The Income Statement can be sent to your account email using the bottom right button on the page.


The Retained Earnings Statement page contains: retained earnings before all transactions, net income, and total dividends. All three are totaled up and returned as the Retained Earnings at the bottom. The Retained Earnings page can be saved or printed with the bottom left button on the page. The Retained Earnings page can be sent to your account email using the bottom right button on the page.


The Balance Sheet has all documented assets, equities, and liabilities. Each has their respective totals then the complete total of equities plus liabilities. The Balance Sheet can be saved or printed with the bottom left button on the page. The Balance Sheet can be sent to your account email using the bottom right button on the page.


The Trial Balance page contains all documented accounts with their type, name, and whether they contain debits or credits. All debits and credits of each account are added up at the bottom of the page. The Trial Balance page can be saved or printed with the bottom left button on the page. The Trial Balance page can be sent to your account email using the bottom right button on the page.



Journaling

Accountants can create journals to document different debits and credits. These journals will come with a place to store comments and files that are related to the journal submitted.


A new journal can be created with up to five different debits and credits for journaling purposes. All journals created will be put in the pending section until an administrator can approve or reject the journal.



My Account

Shows account information about the users account and allows it to be edited. The users profile picture and password can be changed here as well.

Manage Users

Shows a table of user accounts that allow administrators to create users, change information about a user, activate/deactivate users, or delete users.


Allows administrator to add users to Bookkeeping Buddy if necessary. The administrator will have to fill out all the required information for a new user to be created.


Each entry on the user accounts table has an edit button. This will allow administrators to change user account information including the ability to activate or deactivate the account.
